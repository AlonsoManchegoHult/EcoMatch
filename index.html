The Crisis We Can't Ignore

Fast fashion's affordability and speed come at severe environmental and social costs. Gen Z and Millennials use fashion to express identity, yet this unintentionally fuels overconsumption.

Over 100 billion garments are produced annually, contributing to 10% of global carbon emissions, labor exploitation, and overproduction. Social media FOMO drives constant trend cycles.

Sustainable options often feel too expensive or inaccessible. The core challenge: redesign consumption so sustainable choices feel as exciting, validating, and expressive as fast fashion.

What We Discovered

Price vs Values

Consumers are willing to pay 9-12% more for sustainable goods, yet price remains the top barrier.

"I want to shop sustainably but never know what brands are actually ethical."

The FOMO Factor

Fast fashion is driven by FOMO, novelty, instant gratification, and low cost.

"Fast fashion makes me feel stylish, but I hate the guilt."

Trust Issues

Sustainable brands are fragmented; greenwashing creates distrust. Demand exists for transparency.
